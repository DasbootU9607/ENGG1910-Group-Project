ENGG1910 Course Project

AI-Powered Misinformation Risk Assistance for Social Media Users

A reproducible NLP system that ranks political claims by misinformation risk and explains why a claim may deserve verification.

Code Paper PDF Results

Abstract

This project frames misinformation detection as user-facing risk assistance rather than final truth judgment. We evaluate on LIAR, a public benchmark of 12.8K political claims labelled by fact-checkers, and map six truthfulness labels into a binary risk task. A TF-IDF logistic regression baseline achieves 0.743 accuracy, 0.712 F1, and 0.831 ROC-AUC on the official test set. A compact BERT-style transformer provides a deep learning comparison under CPU-limited settings.

Analysis

Ethics and Limitations

The assistant should support verification, not censorship. False positives may unfairly reduce trust in legitimate claims, while false negatives may create over-confidence. A deployed system would need privacy-preserving metadata handling, multilingual evaluation, stronger source reliability signals, and real repost-network analysis.
